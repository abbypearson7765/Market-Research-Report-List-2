<p><p>The High Heat ABS Resin market consists of two main types: VST 100℃~109℃ and VST 110℃~119℃. VST, or Vicat Softening Temperature, refers to the temperature at which a material starts to deform under a specified load. The VST 100℃~109℃ market includes ABS resins that can withstand temperatures ranging from 100℃ to 109℃ without deforming. Similarly, the VST 110℃~119℃ market includes ABS resins that can withstand temperatures ranging from 110℃ to 119℃. These different types cater to various applications that require specific temperature resistances and provide options for customers with varying needs in the high heat ABS resin industry.</p></p>
