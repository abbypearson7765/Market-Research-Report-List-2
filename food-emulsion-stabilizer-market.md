<p><p>The food emulsion stabilizer market includes various types such as xanthan gum, pectin, carboxymethyl cellulose, gum acacia, starch, and others. Xanthan gum is widely used for enhancing the texture and stability of food emulsions. Pectin acts as a thickening agent and stabilizes emulsions by preventing separation. Carboxymethyl cellulose is known for its water retention property, making it suitable for stabilizing emulsions in low-fat products. Gum acacia has excellent emulsifying properties and is used in various food applications. Starch is commonly used as a stabilizer to prevent phase separation in food emulsions. Other types of stabilizers may include hydrocolloids and proteins, aiding in emulsion stability.</p></p>
