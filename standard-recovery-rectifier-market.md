<p><p>Standard recovery rectifiers are electronic devices used in circuits to convert alternating current (AC) into direct current (DC). There are two types of standard recovery rectifiers in the market. Axial standard recovery rectifiers are traditional rectifiers with leads attached to their ends for easy mounting on circuit boards. On the other hand, Surface Mount Device (SMD) standard recovery rectifiers are smaller and more compact, designed for surface mounting directly on the circuit board. Both types serve the same purpose of converting AC to DC but differ in their physical characteristics and mounting methods.</p></p>
