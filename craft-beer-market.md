<p><strong>The Craft Beer Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Ales</li><li>Lagers</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The craft beer market is divided into two main types: ales and lagers. Ales are typically brewed using a top-fermenting yeast at warmer temperatures, resulting in a range of flavors and aromas. They often have a more robust, fruity, and hoppy taste. On the other hand, lagers are brewed with bottom-fermenting yeast at colder temperatures, resulting in a cleaner and crisper taste. They are typically lighter in color and have a smoother, maltier flavor profile. Both types offer a variety of options to cater to different preferences in the craft beer market.</p></p>
