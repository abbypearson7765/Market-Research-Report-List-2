<p><p>Edge Emitting Lasers (EEL) are a type of semiconductor lasers that emit light from the edge of the wafer. The EEL market comprises various types of lasers, including Distributed Feedback Lasers (DFB), Distributed Bragg Reflector Lasers (DBR), Fabry-Perot Lasers (FP), and Broad Area Laser Diodes (BALD). DFB lasers have a grating structure that provides single-mode operation, DBR lasers consist of mirrors to control the emitted wavelength, FP lasers have two mirrors forming a resonant cavity, while BALD lasers have a wide emitting area, enabling high output power.</p></p>
