<p><p>A Negative Temperature Coefficient (NTC) thermistor is a type of resistor whose resistance decreases with an increase in temperature. It is made of a semiconductor material with a negative temperature coefficient, meaning that as the temperature rises, the number of free charge carriers increases, resulting in a decrease in resistance.</p><p>The NTC thermistor market is witnessing steady growth due to several factors. Firstly, the increasing demand for NTC thermistors in the automotive sector for applications such as temperature sensing in engine control units, HVAC systems, and battery management systems is driving market growth. <p><p>Negative temperature coefficient (NTC) thermistors are used in various industries for temperature sensing applications. Different types of NTC thermistors are available in the market to suit different requirements.</p><p>Radial type thermistors have a cylindrical shape and are commonly used in power electronics due to their robustness. Diode type thermistors have a semiconductor diode configuration and provide accurate temperature readings.</p><p>Wire bonding type thermistors are suitable for high-precision applications and are often used in automotive and medical industries. Film type thermistors are made using a thin film of metal, offering stability and durability.</p><p>SMD type thermistors are surface mount devices, ideal for compact, space-constrained applications. Wire type thermistors consist of insulated wire wound into a coil, providing high-temperature stability.</p><p>Chip in glass type thermistors are mounted on a ceramic substrate and covered with glass, offering excellent thermal conductivity. Each type caters to different market needs, providing options for temperature sensing across various industries.</p></p>
