<p><p>RAID cards, also known as Redundant Array of Independent Disks cards, are storage devices used to manage data across multiple hard drives. The market types include RAID 0, which stripes data across drives to enhance performance but without redundancy; RAID 1, where data is mirrored on multiple drives for fault tolerance; RAID 2, rarely used, involves bit-level striping and error correction; RAID 3 employs byte-level striping with a separate parity disk; and finally, other market types encompass RAID 4, RAID 5, RAID 6, and beyond, each offering varying levels of performance, redundancy, and cost.</p></p>
