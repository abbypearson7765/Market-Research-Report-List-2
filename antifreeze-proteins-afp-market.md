<p><p>Antifreeze Proteins (AFP) refer to a group of naturally occurring proteins that have the ability to prevent ice formation and inhibit ice growth. These proteins are found in certain organisms that thrive in extremely cold environments, such as fish, insects, plants, and bacteria. AFPs work by binding to ice crystal surfaces, altering the crystal lattice structure, and lowering the freezing point of water.</p><p>The market analysis of Antifreeze Proteins (AFP) reveals a growing demand in various sectors. The primary application of AFPs is in the food and beverage industry, where they are used as cryoprotectants to prevent ice crystal formation during freezing and thawing processes. This helps in preserving the quality and texture of frozen foods. AFPs are also utilized in the medical field for applications such as cryosurgery, cryopreservation, organ transplantation, and regenerative medicine.</p><p>The Antifreeze Proteins (AFP) Market is expected to witness significant growth in the coming years.
