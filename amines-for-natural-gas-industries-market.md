<p><p>MEA (Monoethanolamine), DEA (Diethanolamine), and MDEA (Methyldiethanolamine) are types of amines commonly used in the natural gas industry. These amines play a vital role in natural gas processing by removing harmful contaminants such as carbon dioxide (CO2) and hydrogen sulfide (H2S) from natural gas streams. MEA is the oldest and most common amine used, while DEA and MDEA are newer and more advanced alternatives. DEA offers improved CO2 removal efficiency, and MDEA provides an even higher level of CO2 and H2S removal, making it highly effective for natural gas purification processes.</p></p>
