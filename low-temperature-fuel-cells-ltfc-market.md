<p><p>Low Temperature Fuel Cells (LTFC) market types include Alkaline Fuel Cells (AFC), Phosphoric Acid Fuel Cells (PAFC), Molten Carbonate Fuel Cells (MCFC), and Others. AFCs utilize potassium hydroxide as an electrolyte, enabling high energy density and efficiency. PAFCs use phosphoric acid as the electrolyte, offering stability and reliability. MCFCs utilize carbonate salts as the electrolyte, providing high efficiency and tolerance to impurities. Other LTFC types encompass Solid Oxide Fuel Cells (SOFC) and Direct Methanol Fuel Cells (DMFC), each with unique advantages such as high temperature operation and low-cost materials.</p></p>
